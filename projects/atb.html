Around the Block

Roles: Frontend Lead, Developer, Researcher

With a team of three, I designed and developed a website for artists struggling with creative block, looking to challenge themselves and connect with a community of creatives. Users can generate random art challenges, called meChallenges, that will challenge them to create something new and unique every time. Users can also participate in community challenges, called weChallenges, and see what other artists are creating. Users can post submissions for the challenges and get feedback and support from other members of the community.

01. Research

The Problem

A lot of artists struggle with creative block. One way artists overcome this is by using idea generators. There are definitely existing art prompt generators, but they don't really facilitate a sense of community or the sharing of art. While there have been art prompts and art challenges such as Inktober in the past, it is often difficult to view the work of other artists from these challenges in an organized manner.

We wanted to create a space where artists can find inspiration from prompts as well as other artists' work. At the same time, we wanted to avoid the pitfall of social media in which artists become too focused on engagement rather than art itself.

User Interviews and Personas

Before starting the design process, our team administered 12 user interviews with artists, of which I conducted 6. From the information we gathered, we created 3 user personas to represent the different types of users that our website targets. We also created empathy maps to identiy user pain points and journey maps to visualize use cases.
